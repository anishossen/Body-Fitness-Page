Some Exercise Guidlines

Bicycle Crunch

Bicycle Crunch exercise involves multiple muscle groups and includes a cardio component, it can help burn calories and improve cardiovascular health.Bicycle crunches target the abdominal muscles, including the rectus (the "six-pack" muscle), obliques, and transverse , helping to strengthen and tone the core.Incorporating this type of exercise into your routine can contribute to a stronger, more toned midsection, improved athletic performance, and better posture.

Hip Flexor Stretch

Hip Flexor Stretch, Flexible hip flexors can enhance performance in activities that require hip extension, such as running, jumping, and other athletic movements. Stretching the hip flexors reduces the risk of injuries related to muscle imbalances or tightness.This exercise is particularly beneficial for those who spend a lot of time sitting or those who engage hello Regularly incorporating this stretch into your routine can help maintain flexibility and prevent discomfort.

Push - Up

Push up Targets the chest (pectorals), shoulders (deltoids), and triceps. Engages the biceps and the muscles of the upper back.Activates the core muscles, including the abdominals and lower back, helping to improve overall stability and balance.Can be modified to increase or decrease difficulty (e.g., by elevating feet for more challenge or performing on knees for less intensity). This exercise is effective for building upper body strength, improving endurance, and enhancing overall body stability.

Lunge

Lunge Targets the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves, making it an excellent exercise for building lower body strength.Mimics daily activities like walking, running, and climbing stairs, making it beneficial for functional fitness.Involves the core to maintain balance, thereby strengthening the abdominal and lower back muscles. This exercise is great for building lower body strength, improving balance, and enhancing overall mobility and flexibility.
